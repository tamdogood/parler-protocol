# Artwork guide

Parler's campaign is dark, tactile, and editorial. It treats a conversation as a continuous ribbon
of ordered context rather than a stream of glowing data. Vellum makes the context feel readable and
durable; satin gives it motion; stone thresholds and brass rings make handoff and approval feel
deliberate.

The result should look at home in a design magazine before it looks like a developer-tool ad. There
are no fake terminals, neon grids, literal keys, robots, or glowing brains.

## Crop and overlay rules

- Preserve the ribbon's entry and exit when the story is handoff. A crop should not turn it into an
  unexplained floating object.
- Keep at least 6 percent padding around the focal object or threshold.
- Put overlay copy in existing near-black negative space. Do not add a gradient veil over the art or
  cover the ribbon.
- Use warm off-white text. Use muted violet or sea-glass green for one small rule, underline, or CTA.
- Use one headline and, at most, one URL. Let the artwork carry the feature metaphor.
- Use the portrait approval asset for 4:5 feeds and the square assets for 1:1 placements instead of
  forcing a wide crop.

## Generation direction

The final images were created with the built-in image generation tool. Use the following common
brief before the feature-specific line when extending the set:

```text
Premium dark editorial installation art for Parler Protocol. Use a continuous translucent vellum
and satin ribbon as the metaphor for ordered conversation context. Near-black architectural space,
matte stone, blackened brass, warm ivory paper, muted ink blue, dusty violet, and sea-glass green.
Low-key museum lighting, tactile natural grain, calm luxury-magazine art direction, strong silhouette,
and generous negative space. No readable text, logos, people, robots, brains, clouds, computers,
code, UI screens, neon grids, circuit boards, literal keys, padlocks, shields, or watermarks. Avoid
cyberpunk, gaming renders, generic network diagrams, corporate 3D icons, and excessive bloom.
```

Feature-specific concepts:

- **Conversation handoff:** the ribbon carries four message panels between two architectural openings and
  passes through one understated circular threshold without breaking.
- **Team session:** one ordered ribbon becomes three graceful branches; each branch passes through
  its own brass approval ring before reaching a distinct alcove.
- **Join approval:** the ribbon pauses at an arched doorway and a brass ring; light is visible beyond,
  but the conversation has not crossed yet.
- **Local mode:** the ribbon loops entirely inside one circular room with no opening to the outside.
- **Memory:** an ordered vellum archive rests in stone while the ribbon lifts exactly one leaf into
  focus.
- **Signed identity:** distinct blind-embossed cards carry seals made from their own material, with no
  generic lock or shield metaphor.
- **Code/file handoff:** a layered vellum portfolio rides the ribbon between two stone plinths and
  arrives with its repeated embossed mark intact.

## What not to make

- A humanoid robot holding a laptop.
- A glowing brain connected to generic nodes.
- A purple-gradient SaaS dashboard with three feature cards.
- A handshake, clipboard, puzzle piece, cloud, key, lock, or shield as the main metaphor.
- A neon grid, terminal wall, code screenshot, or circuit board used as visual shorthand for
  "technical."
- Security art that implies message encryption from the hub operator.
